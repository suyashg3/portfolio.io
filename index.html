About Me

Hello! I’m Suyash Gadhave, a Physical Design Engineer passionate about VLSI backend design, timing closure, and sign-off methodologies. With hands-on experience in RTL-to-GDSII flow, I have worked across 7nm, 14nm, and 28nm technology nodes, focusing on timing optimization, congestion resolution, and power integrity improvements.

Currently, I’m part of Pragmatic Silicon, where I execute physical design and STA methodologies, ensuring 100% DRC/LVS clean designs. Previously, I trained at VLSIGURU Training Institute, specializing in multi-voltage domain designs and clock tree synthesis. Additionally, during my internship at GE Healthcare, I worked on FPGA design migration, RTL updates, and automation scripting, contributing to 30% efficiency improvements in verification processes.

I hold an M.Tech in Microelectronics from Manipal Institute of Technology and a B.E. in Instrumentation & Control. My expertise spans physical design methodologies, ASIC design, power distribution, and clock optimization, with strong skills in TCL, Python, and Shell scripting.

Beyond work, I actively contribute to VLSI research, having published conference papers on Multiply-Accumulate Units for CNNs and Low-Power CNN Design. I am always eager to explore new methodologies that enhance VLSI design efficiency and push the boundaries of semiconductor innovation.
